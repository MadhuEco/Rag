## Buying coffee with a contactless card
A Customer and a Barista are both Business Actors assigned to the Coffee Purchase Service. The customer orders and the barista fulfils both roles are necessary for the service to exist, so both get an Assignment arrow into it.
The Order & Pay Business Process is what actually happens: the customer taps their card, the terminal beeps, and coffee is handed over. That process is composed of a Coffee Order (what was ordered and for how much) and triggers a separate Payment Authorisation Service, which is owned by the external Payment Network actor. This is a Triggering relationship  the order process kicks off auth as a consequence, not a sub-step it controls.
On the Application layer, the POS Application realizes the Coffee Purchase Service (it is the software that makes that service possible), and the Payment Gateway App realizes the Payment Authorisation Service. The POS Application is composed of a Take Payment Application Service, which in turn serves the Order & Pay process upward into the Business layer. The Payment Gateway App produces a Transaction Record (Data Object) that flows up to become the Payment Transaction Business Object the record that matters for receipts and reconciliation.
On the Technology layer, the NFC Card Reader (Device) is assigned to the POS Application it is the physical hardware the app runs on at the point of tap. The POS Terminal Server (Node) is assigned to the POS Application as the compute node hosting it. The Network Communication Service (Technology Service) serves both the Payment Gateway App (routing auth requests to the network) and the Payment Network actor directly.
Every arrow has a one-sentence justification. No Application element appears on the Business layer. No Technology element appears on the Application layer.
